About

I’ve always loved engineering. As a kid, it was the rush—snap two things together, see if they work, sprint to show someone. That spark never left. Read more →

Over time, that spark grew up. I learned engineering isn’t just about making things—it’s about solving real problems: taking principles off the whiteboard and turning them into systems that actually help people.

During my internship at an HVAC firm, something clicked: I don’t just think in steps—I think in systems. I challenge defaults. I look for connections others miss. That mindset sharpened at SmithGroup, where I presented an integrated farm concept: crops and a hen coop working as one loop so waste becomes input and nothing is idle. It wasn’t about buzzwords; it was about balance—energy, airflow, water, labor—designed to cooperate.

That’s the thread through my work: playful curiosity fused with real-world grit. I start at the root cause, then pull ideas from anywhere they fit—HVAC for thermal control, manufacturing for repeatability, ag-tech for resilience, controls for feedback—and shape them into one coherent system. If it survives dust, heat, budgets, and humans, it’s a win.

You’ll see that philosophy across this portfolio: a field-repairable mini tractor, a modular rice parboiler with heat recovery, a capstone dish-drying system for reusable takeout, and reliability work in industry. Different domains, same approach—design for the messy world, not the lab.

Mechanical engineering is my palette; a world full of hard problems is my canvas; the systems I design are my masterpieces-in-progress. If you’re building things that must work outside a slide deck, we’ll get along.
